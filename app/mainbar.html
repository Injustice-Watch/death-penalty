400 Years of Executions
By Sam Hart
April 23, 2017

The Arkansas Department of Corrections begins a series of seven executions today, aiming to deliver the death penalty to seven death row inmates over the course of the next eleven days: an unprecedented number of executions in such a narrow window of time. The state is rushing to complete the executions because of the expiration of its supply of controversial lethal injection drug, midazolam.

According to the Death Penalty Info Center, this will be the first time in 17 years that two people were executed in the same state on the same day, the most recent being Texas in the year 2000. Arkansas’s last double execution happened in 1999.

Other numbers we found interesting:

  • 4,711 inmates have been executed in states where the death penalty is no longer legal.
  • Of nearly 16,000 total executions, only 372 inmates (2.36%) executed were women.
  • 7,316 inmates (48%) executed were black.
